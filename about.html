Our Story

A rookie FRC team years in the making.

Built for the Moment

Robotics they can actually do.

Robotics sounds intimidating until a girl sees someone who looks like her actually doing it — building something, explaining how it works, not just watching. That changes things fast, and VOLT was built for that moment and for everything that comes next.

Our rookie FRC team grew from two all-girls FTC programs, Polytechnic Puzzle Pieces #16751 and Gear Girls #23469. This season, 15 students carried that work forward onto our official FRC roster, drawn from a combined program of 25 students representing seven schools across the O'Fallon area.

Student-Led

The students run things.

To make the jump from FTC to FRC, we built out student-led subteams across every discipline. Mentors are there, but the students lead. If something goes wrong, they figure out why. If it works, they know exactly what they did to make it happen.

🤖

Robot Build

Design/CAD, Electrical, Mechanical, Instrumentation & Integration, Software, and Maintenance & Testing.

📋

Business

Outreach & Community Engagement, Documentation, Scouting & Research, Safety, Media & Marketing, Finance, and Project Management.

🏆

Captains

Robot and Business each have a student captain, and each subteam has its own — students can join multiple teams but captain only one.

Daily operations run on Pumble, Google Docs, Canva, the BAND app, and Onshape for CAD.

Our Approach

Master the fundamentals first.

Rather than building beyond our experience too quickly, we started from a proven starter-bot foundation and improved it from there. That let our students focus first on the basics: building something reliable, wiring it safely, getting real drive practice, and understanding how FRC works as a team — while building tools and processes that carry into next season.
